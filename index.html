Top Market Storylines

1. AI Rally Continues Unabated

The AI-fueled tech rally showed no signs of slowing last week, with Nvidia leading the charge after announcing a new chip architecture. The semiconductor giant's stock surged nearly 9% on the week, pushing its market cap past $1 trillion. Meanwhile, other AI beneficiaries like Microsoft and Alphabet also posted solid gains as investors continue to bet big on the transformative potential of artificial intelligence across industries.

2. Fed Pause Expected Amid Cooling Inflation

With CPI data due Tuesday and the Fed meeting Wednesday, all eyes are on inflation trends and monetary policy. Recent data suggests price pressures are easing, with core PCE falling to 4.6% year-over-year in the last reading. Markets are pricing in a 92% chance of a pause this month, according to CME FedWatch, but will be closely parsing Powell's comments for clues about the July meeting and potential second-half trajectory.

3. Tesla's Price War Weighs on Margins

Tesla shares dropped over 4% last week as analysts expressed concerns about eroding margins from the company's aggressive price cuts. The EV maker has slashed prices six times this year in an effort to boost demand and fend off competition, but this strategy appears to be coming at a cost. Investors will be watching delivery numbers due next week for signs that the cuts are having their intended effect on volumes.

Closing Thoughts

This week's CPI data and Fed decision could set the tone for summer trading. While the AI trade remains red-hot, some analysts warn of stretched valuations in the tech sector. Meanwhile, traditional value sectors like energy and financials continue to lag, creating potential opportunities for contrarian investors. With quarter-end approaching, expect some portfolio rebalancing flows that could add volatility to the mix.
